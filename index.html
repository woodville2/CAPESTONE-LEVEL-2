How to Fix a Flat Tire

  • Deflate the tire completely
  • Remove the wheel from the bicycle
  • Use tire levers to detach the tire from the rim
  • Remove the inner tube
  • Patch or replace the tube
  • Reinstall the tube and tire
  • Inflate the tire

Adjust Your Own Brakes

  • Adjust Brake Cable Tension:
  • Locate the cable connecting the brake lever to the caliper
  • Loosen the bolt holding the cable
  • Pull the cable tighter or loosen it as needed
  • Retighten the bolt and test the brake lever feel
  • Brake Pads:
  • Check Brake Pads inspect for wear and replace if necessary
  • Squeeze the brake lever to bring pads close to the rim
  • Adjust pad position for even spacing on both sides
  • Release and check alignment
  • Test the Brakes: Squeeze the lever to ensure smooth engagement without rim rubbing
  • Fine-Tune as Necessary: Repeat adjustments until you achieve desired performance

Maintain the Chain

Cleaning:

  • Frequency: Clean regularly, especially after wet or muddy rides
  • Methods:
    • On-bike cleaning: Use a brush or chain cleaner tool while the chain is on the bike
    • Off-bike cleaning: Remove the chain and soak in soapy water or degreaser
  • Rinse: Thoroughly rinse with water to remove cleaning solution residue
